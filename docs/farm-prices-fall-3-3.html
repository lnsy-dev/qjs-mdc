U.S. farm prices slipped in January as lower prices for milk, cattle and major row crops pulled down a key government index, even as some specialty crops and poultry strengthened, the U.S. Department of Agriculture reported Friday. release.nass.usda

Farm prices move lower

The index of prices received by farmers for all agricultural commodities fell 3.3% in January from December and was down 10% from January 2025, according to USDA’s National Agricultural Statistics Service Agricultural Prices report. The all-crops index declined 2.9% on the month, while the all-livestock and products index dropped 3.6%, signaling broad weakness across much of the farm economy. release.nass.usda

Producers received lower prices in January for milk, cattle, corn, soybeans and wheat, which more than offset higher prices for broilers, lettuce and several specialty crops. The food commodities index, which tracks prices for items such as grains, oilseeds, livestock and dairy used directly in the food supply, fell 3.6% from December and was 13% below a year earlier. release.nass.usda

Crop prices under pressure

Average prices for key row crops moved mostly lower compared with both the previous month and a year earlier. The national average corn price received by producers in January declined from December and stood well below the January 2025 level, while soybeans and all wheat also registered year-over-year decreases. release.nass.usda

USDA reported that rice prices were relatively steadier, supported by tight supplies in some regions and firm export demand, even as grain and oilseed prices overall remained under peaks reached during the 2022–23 period. Prices for some fruits, vegetables and hay strengthened on seasonal supply tightness, partially offsetting declines in broad field crop categories. release.nass.usda

Livestock and dairy weaken

Livestock producers also faced softer markets in January. Cattle and milk prices fell from December, contributing to declines in the cattle, milk and all-livestock indexes, while hog prices showed less month-to-month movement but remained under last year’s levels. release.nass.usda

Broiler prices posted a modest gain, helping to offset some of the weakness in red meat and dairy categories and providing a bright spot for poultry producers. The mixed picture across animal agriculture underscored how shifting consumer demand and export patterns are affecting different proteins unevenly. release.nass.usda

Margins stay tight

Prices paid by farmers for production items and services, interest, taxes and wage rates were little changed in January, leaving the overall parity ratio — a measure comparing prices received to prices paid — below its year-earlier level. That gap underscores continued margin pressure for many producers as commodity prices ease faster than costs for fuel, labor and other inputs. release.nass.usda

Analysts watch the monthly Agricultural Prices data closely because it feeds into farm income projections, crop insurance benchmarks and future policy discussions. The next Agricultural Prices report, due in late March, will update February price movements as producers advance spring planting decisions in a cooler but still volatile farm economy. esmis.nal.usda
